Overview

The Har Ki Dun Trek is a journey back in time. Located in the cradle-shaped Har Ki Dun Valley in Uttarkashi, this trek is as much about cultural immersion and mythology as it is about natural grandeur. The trail follows the glacial river Tons through pine forests, ancient villages, and alpine meadows before culminating at Har Ki Dun and the sacred Maninda Tal

With moderate difficulty, gentle ascents, and well-maintained trails, Har Ki Dun is perfect for beginners, families, and photographers. The trek blends forest charm, mountain serenity, and historical stories in a way few treks can.

Trek Highlights

  • Ancient Valley Trail: Walk on one of the oldest trekking routes in India, once used by shepherds and traders.
  • Breathtaking Himalayan Landscapes: Witness mesmerizing views of snow-clad peaks like Swargarohini, Bandarpoonch, and Black Peak.
  • Alpine Lakes & Meadows: Explore the sacred Maninda Tal and lush alpine meadows bursting with wildflowers.
  • Mythological Significance: : Linked to the Mahabharata, it's believed the Pandavas took this route to reach heaven.
  • Rich Culture & Wildlife: Experience the charm of wooden villages, traditional Garhwali hospitality, and chances to spot Himalayan birds and wildlife.
